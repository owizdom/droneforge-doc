The **Community** tab is the public catalog of Nimbus agents. Browse, fork (download), or publish your own.

## What's there

Each entry in the Community tab is a published agent. Listings include:

- The agent's name and a short description.
- The author (your DroneForge account).
- Hardware requirements (which FC variants it expects, which sensors it uses).
- Vision modes it requires (YOLOv11 / DepthAnything / both / neither).
- Tags / category (search-and-land, follow, racing, etc.).

## Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find an agent">
    Filter by hardware compatibility, running on a BetaFPV Whoop, you can filter to agents that work with `STM32G47X_BETAFPVG473`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download">
    Pulls the agent into your sidebar in the Build tab. From there it's editable like anything you authored, make a copy and modify.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish your own">
    From your local agents in the Build tab, click Publish. The app uploads:
    - The agent's JSON.
    - Hardware requirements.
    - Description and tags you provide.

    Demonstrations from the Flight tab are **not** included unless you explicitly add them.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why community matters

The agent format is small and human-readable. A working Nimbus Map for a task like *"track and land on a person's outstretched hand"* is on the order of a few hundred tokens of expression code. Once one person solves it, anyone with a Nimbus can pull and run it, agents are portable across drones, so a graph that works on a Whoop generally works on an Axis F7 with at most parameter adjustments.

## Etiquette

- **Test before publishing.** Other users will fly your agent. If it has a clear failure mode, document it in the description.
- **Cite hardware requirements honestly.** An agent that needs optical flow won't work on a drone without the BF fork's Micolink driver. List it.
- **Don't publish unsafe defaults.** `aux1 = 1800` from tick zero arms the moment Play is pressed. That's fine for an experienced pilot, dangerous for a beginner. Note it in the description.
